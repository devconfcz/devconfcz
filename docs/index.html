About DevConf.cz

DevConf.cz 2017 is the 9th annual, free and open 3-day open-source Fedora Linux and JBoss community conference for Red Hat and community developers, DevOps, testers, and documentation writers, organized to sync, share, and hack on upstream projects with the community in the beautiful city of Brno, Czech Republic.

You should consider attending DevConf.cz 2017 if you are:

  • A technology architect or developer
  • An IT consultant
  • An IT student or a teacher from an IT university/faculty
  • Or simply an IT enthusiast interested in the latest trends in open source and emerging digital technologies

If you're curious about last year's event, you can check out DevConf.cz 2016 schedule and videos on our DevConf.cz YouTube Channel.

Same as last year, there is NO REGISTRATION FEE for participation at the conference.

However, REGISTRATION (FREE) WILL BE REQUIRED this year! Stay tuned for more details on how to register!

Language of the conference: English

Session Types

  • Keynotes (40m+10m) – Powerful talks by high-profile contributors that establish the main underlying event theme
  • Technical Talk (20m+5m | 40m+10m) – demonstration, lecture, or speech meant to inform, persuade, or build good will
    • Instructional – teaches a topic
    • Case Study – explains a real-life implementation and its outcome
    • Inspirational – makes you think about something differently
    • Marketing – introduces a product | library | framework | etc
  • Workshop / Lab (110m | 160m) – tutorial, seminar, discussion group that emphasizes exchange of ideas and the demonstration and application of techniques, skills, etc

Difficulty Levels

To help us set expectations with attendees, make sure to set an appropriate level of the difficulty for your session. For beginner talks, you shouldn't expect the audience to know anything more than the name of the subject you're talking about. For all other difficulty ratings, please include in your proposal what you expect the audience should know before coming into the talk, just to be sure it's absolutely clear.

  • Beginner – No experience needed to participate
  • Intermediate – Attendees should be familiar with the subject
  • Advanced – Extensive background knowlege required

Timeslots / Breaks

Breaks: Every session, including break, will either end on an even 30m or 60m interval. Concretely:
  • 5m|10m – Lightning Talk
  • 20m + 5m – half-hour (30 minute) block, with 5m buffer / break between sessions
  • 40m + 10m – standard 1 hour (60 minute) talk block, with 10m break
  • 100m + 20m – standard 2 hour (120 minute) workshop block, with 20m break
  • 160m – extended 3 hour (180 minute) workshop block, with 20m break

Additional Guidelines

For a greater chance of being accepted, please make sure your proposal includes the following. Your proposal will not be evaluated without adequate information:

  • Select ONE track that best fits with subject matter at hand; use OTHER if none seem to fit
  • Provide as much detail directly in your abstract as possible, ie
    • don't expect people to follow links to external resources
    • provide an outline and thorough description of what the session will cover
  • Describe why you think the topic is important for the audience
  • Note background information that expresses why you are qualified to present on the topic
  • Explain what you expect the audience will learn from the session
  • Make the title short and concise (under 70 characters)
  • Provide direct links to external resources the audience must be familar with or download before attending

Session Submission Process

Ready to submit your session proposal to the DevConf.cz 2017 CfP? Just follow these instructions.

  1. Propose and submit your presentation from 'Submit an Abstract' button below by November 11th, 2016
  2. All submissions will be reviewed by the DevConf.CZ program panel
  3. You will receive an email from informing your that your talk was either ACCEPTED or REJECTED by December 2nd, 2016
    • Sorry! We can't accept everyone's talks; may the best talks be accepted!
    • All tracks will have at least two alternat selected too, in case of last minute cancellations
  4. Before Christmas the tentative schedule will be ready. You will be asked to review for any problems or last minute updates
  5. In the first week of January the final schedule will be posted publically
  6. There may be additional emails sync asynchronously about scheduling and miscellaneous questions from participants.
  7. A followup email will be posted after DevConf.CZ 2017 ends with information about posting presentation materials.
